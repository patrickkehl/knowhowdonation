Know-How Donation Crowdfunding Campaign

Overview of the Crowdfunding ICO

The present initial coin offering (ICO) of the know-how donation (KHDON) token serves the funding of a non-profit foundation and audit organization. Participation in the ICO is a donation for the creation of the needed legal entities and following the road map as it is described in the White paper: “Know-How Transfer Paradigm Change.

How to participate

In short, just send before Wednesday, May 1, 2019 at 10:37:13 UTC some ETH to the ethereum contract address 0x6A5E59fBaeA3E07737fBAFCeb600CBDe5a295F24.

Explanation of the KHDON crowdfunding campaign

The ethereum network allows to program a contract that can receive ether (ETH) - a cryptocurrency that is traded worldwide in exchange of to national currencies. The crowdfunding is facilitated by two such contracts. The first contract defines the Know-How Donation Token as a cryptocurrency and the second contract initiates a crowdsale of this token.

All contract code modules are coming from the OpenZeppelin. These code modules are audited and make the contracts more secure. The following modules are central to the crowdfunding campaign:

RefundableCrowdsale - set a minimal goal to be reached and returns funds if it’s not met. MintedCrowdsale - tokens are minted in each purchase.

The code of the KHDON Token and KHDON Crowdsale contracts is public and the contracts are verified against the code.

It basically works like this: When ether is transferred to the KHDON Crowdsale contract, then the KHDON token is minted by the KHDON Token contract and sent as a receipt to the sender. The ether is collected in a refund escrow until the end of the crowdsale. The full escrow is sent to the contract owner if the escrow is higher than the crowdsale goal. Otherwise every sender can request a refund directly at the contract and the contract sends the ether back.

The KHDON Crowdsale has the following settings:

Token name Know-How Donation Token

Exchange rate 10 KHDON / 1 ETH
Minimal goal to be met 500 ETH
Crowdsale start Saturday, October 13, 2018 at 10:37:43 UTC
Crowdsale end Wednesday, May 1, 2019 at 10:37:13 UTC

When you send 1 ETH to the contract address 0x6A5E59fBaeA3E07737fBAFCeb600CBDe5a295F24 until May 1, 2019, then you will receive 10 KHDON as a receipt. The ether will be kept by the contract in an escrow and only released to me when the minimal goal of 500 ETH is surpassed till the end of the donation campaign. When the goal is not met, then you can simply call the refund function of the contract and get your donation back.

Why so complicated?

The basis of technology needed for timed know-how donations is already here and used by crypocurrencies such as bitcoin and ethereum. Many people are already using this technology actively. For these people participating in an ICO on the ethereum blockchain is easier than initiating a bank transfer.

The refundable crowdsale is a means to ensure that a minimal funding is achieved or all donations can be reclaimed. The goal is equivalent to the minimal funding of an international foundation in Switzerland required by authorities - I do not want to have any donations on my bank account if this amount is not met and the project cannot move forward at this time.

In any case, the gained publicity makes the crowdfunding campaign a success. The primary goal is to spread the idea of timed know-how donations and stimulate reflections on know-how life cycles as well as know-how distribution. Some ideas need longer time to root in society. However, if there is a chance to initiate a know-how transfer paradigm change now - I will not hesitate a second to do so. Clearly the best outcome would not be a minimal funding, but a funding that allows the build up of global operations.
