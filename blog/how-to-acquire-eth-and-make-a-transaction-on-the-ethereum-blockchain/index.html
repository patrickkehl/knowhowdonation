How to acquire ETH and make a transaction on the ethereum blockchain

Let's go step by step into how to get an account on MetaMask, acquire some real ether and make your first transactions on the ethereum blockchain.

Basics

Some things work a little bit different in the crypto world. You need for a start an account address on the ethereum network and you do not depend on other people to get the address. No bank will make an account for you. All you need is a sequence of 12 words that will create and unlock your account. The sequence of 12 words is a mnemonic that is much easier to deal with than a cryptic combination of random letters. These 12 worlds need to be random in order to be safe. The human brain can easily remember 12 words - but even better if they are not random. The words are not random For example if you choose any 12 words from this website or a favorite book of yours. Even if you invent a story consisting of 12 words, the words are not random. The problem with non-random words is the following: Anyone skilled could write a program to test combinations of 12 words to unlock the blockchain. It is just a matter of computing time and skill of the programmer until he hits a combination of 12 words that someone thought easy to remember. An approach could be to use ebooks of recent bestseller books as input and check if anyone in this world though this a good hiding place for a mnemonic. Obviously it is no good hiding place. A random combination of 12 words is safe because all the calculation power of this world is not enough to test all possible combinations against the blockchain until the end of the universe. When a human chooses seemingly random words, then this level of security is not achieved. The human brain might like combination of words that are easier to remember. This means a brute force attack would not need to go on until the end of the universe. The more logic the choices are for you, the shorter the computation time required to break into your account.

I hope with this I have convinced you that mnemonics are safe, but you need a program to choose the words randomly for you. And this program should store the mnemonic only encrypted. To store the words in plain text or in a picture is not recommendable - anyone who might get access to your computer would also get access to your account.

MetaMask installation

MetaMask is a program that can choose a random mnemonic for you, store it in encrypted form and allow you to access the ethereum blockchain with this mnemonic. You need to have Chrome or Firefox installed.

Now head over to metamask.io

and get the chrome extension.

The rights for the extension are OK. After all the program needs to make the payments for you.

Now you are ready. There is no need to sync to other devices. You can just close the pop-up window.

Anytime you need to open MetaMask, just click on the fox icon in the upper right corner. On the first opening you have to choose if you want to go with a standard version or with a beta version. Choose the beta version. It works fine and gives you more control.

Now you need to set up the program. Just a remark before we start. You can do everything in an extended view or in a pop-up view. If you close by accident the tab with MetaMask in the browser, you can return to the extended view anytime by clicking on the icon to open the pop-up view. Then you press the account options represented by tree dots and select “Extended View”.

Choose a secure password, something much better than “12345678”. This password secures your access to MetaMask and the mnemonic of your account.

Now you see your account picture. Out of your mnemonic a unique picture has been created. By time you will remember this picture. It is a help to visualize on which account you are or to which account you are sending the cryptocurrency ether.

Please read the terms of use.

After scrolling down you can accept them.

Ethereum is a public blockchain. This means anyone can see the amount of ether you hold on your account. But only the people to whom you give your account address know this is your account.

Read the warning carefully. Never unveil your mnemonic when prompted. Actually you should only unveil it when you do the setup and leave it covered from then on.

Now it gets interesting. Behind this screen you find your mnemonic.

This means you really need to store this mnemonic safely. I leave it up to you how you would like to define storing safely. For the moment I am assuming you will just store a small amount on the account. A piece of paper is therefore fine to store the mnemonic. The account is empty and you need to buy ether (ETH) before you can make a transaction. Ether is a cryptocurrency with a limited supply and all ether is already in circulation. There is no recreation or minting of ether taking place. This is in contrast to the real money, where every country will print money as needed. And another difference is that no country is accepting cryptocurrency as official money, even when some treat them as a foreign currency. This means ether has only value from the people giving it a value. You are one of these people giving it a value. If you want to have a tiny amount of ether, you need to offer real money until someone else is willing to give you ether. Luckily, you don’t need to search for someone with ether and make a bid personally. There are many cryptocurrency exchanges established over the whole world. If there are price differences between them someone trades between them and the differences get smaller. From a practical point of view there roughly on actual price for ether, but you loose money in fees when you trade on an exchange. There is always a small spread between the buy and sell price, as with an real money foreign exchange. The actual price you can check at CoinMarketCap. There you can also see how much the value is fluctuating. If more people want to use ETH the price will raise and it will fall if less people want to use it.

Let’s do something more complicated - like sending ETH to a smart contract. Behind a smart contract is not person with a mnemonic but code. The smart contract will do what it is programmed to do. You need to find out more about the smart contract and what is behind it. Usually there is a homepage telling you more about it.

The indicated transaction price to the smart contract address is much higher than the one mnemonic address. The reason behind is simple. The smart contract requires more storage and computing power on the decentralized blockchain. Therefore, the costs are higher. However the gas fee of 0.038 ETH indicated represents more maximal transaction price. Click on the slider next to the Gas Fee to learn more.

The 0.038 ETH gas fee are coming from the multiplication of gas prize of 5GWEI times gas limit of 7600000. There comes an analogy handy when a transaction is launched it is like a truck delivering some goods. Before the truck can start he must get gasoline at a gas station. The gasoline has a gas prize and the tank a limiting volume. The truck cannot tank more gasoline than the volume of the tank. The tank is empty before the truck starts the delivery. Therefore, the truck driver needs to pay at the gas station to fill the tank up. He needs to pay the gasoline prize times the volume. The higher the gasoline prize the more he needs to pay. However, when the truck driver returns after his delivery he will still have some gasoline left. For this gasoline he gets a refund. Therefore, the transaction price is lower than the gas fee to confirm before the truck driver starts his tour. You can change the gas prize and the gas limit manually. Don’t lower the gas limit. You only risk the the truck driver runs out of fuel before he finished the delivery. This would mean, no delivery of the ETH to the contract, but full consumption of the gasoline. However, you could look for a cheaper gas station. Open ETH Gas Station in a separate tab.

Every transaction needs to confirmed by someone running a full ethereum installation. This confirmation is called mining as the miners gets a tiny little amount from the confirmations. It is a a bit like mining gold. You pay with the gas fee the inscription of the transaction to the blockchain. The gas fee is an incentive for all Miners to let their computers work on the task and it pays for their running costs. Naturally, miners prefer higher payed transactions with a higher gas prize. This means a transaction with a lower gas prize will have more trouble to get confirmed by the miners. The value of the gas prize depends on the traffic in the ethereum network. You see on the ETH gas station what lower limit of gas prize that has good chance to be confirmed within 5 min. Right now the this lower limit gas prize is 2.1 GWEI. You can lower the gas prize manually with the risk that the transaction will be written to the blockcain. This can happen if the reward for the miners is too low and they always have higher prized transactions in the queue. I would not recommend to go lower than the save low <30 min gas prize limit.

If you send now a transaction with a gas prize of 2.5 GWEI, the waiting time till confirmation should not be too bad. We have all information needed, you can close the tab again and update the Customize Gas settings.

Summary

By now you should be familiar with making basic transactions on the ethereum network. I hope you enjoyed the ride.

The step-by-step guide should allow you to get started to make transactions to any ethereum address. I used an account address of mine and the one of the know-how donation crowdfunding campaign as an example. If you like the concept behind timed know-how donations, I would be pleased if you participate in the crowdfunding campaign.

Otherwise just replace the addresses by your own choice. I strongly recommend that you make some research before you use a new address and you double check that you copied the right address before you make a transaction. You need to verify who is behind an address and in case of contracts what the contract is doing. This is a precaution to avoid loosing ether. A contract could for example have an error in the code so that no one can ever retrieve again the transferred ether. People test the contract code before deployment, but you need to gain confidence before make a transaction. No one can give you guarantees and you are responsible for your own actions. These are just little precautionary remarks to help you. Don’t let yourself be discouraged.

If you dare to make your own responsible steps you will gain the ability to transfer value in a decentralized blockchain. And how cool is that! Your transactions are written into a blockchain that is available to anyone, that has not one location but many and is independent of any nation. The technology allows many new ideas. Anything is possible on the ethereum network and it is a wonderful world to explore. Let yourself be inspired and good luck with your first steps.
